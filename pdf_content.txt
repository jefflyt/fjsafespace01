1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW The FJ SafeSpace Healthy Workplace Program is an independent indoor air quality (IAQ) verification framework designed to assess and demonstrate healthier indoor environments in workplaces. The program converts IAQ measurements into: • Clear IAQ benchmarks • A FJ SafeSpace Wellness Index™ score • A recognised verification outcome This enables organisations to demonstrate environmental quality and transparency to employees, visitors, and stakeholders. The framework references internationally recognised benchmarks: • SS 554:2016 – Indoor Air Quality for Air-Conditioned Buildings (Singapore) • WELL Building Standard v2 – Performance Verification Guidebook (Q4 2022) • Alignment with BCA Green Mark 2021 Health & Wellbeing criteria Verification follows the SAFE-AIR™ Protocol.

5. SENSOR DEPLOYMENT GUIDELINES SENSOR DENSITY Area Size Recommended Sensors ≤100 m² 1 sensor 100–300 m² 2 sensors 300–600 m² 3 sensors PLACEMENT PRINCIPLES Sensors should be placed: • at breathing height (1.1–1.5 m) • away from supply vents • away from windows and doors • not directly beside pollutant sources • within representative occupant zones Avoid placing sensors: • in direct sunlight • directly beside printers or kitchen areas unless specifically assessing those zones  
6. IAQ BENCHMARK MATRIX The program references both: • SS 554:2016 (Singapore indoor air standard) • WELL Building Standard v2 Where limits differ, the stricter threshold is applied. DATA ANALYSIS METHOD Sensors collect minute-level environmental data. Analysis typically includes: • average values • peak values • exceedance events • daily patterns and trends This prevents incorrect interpretation caused by short-term fluctuations.  
8. FJ SAFESPACE WELLNESS INDEX™ To make IAQ results understandable to non-technical audiences, environmental data is translated into a Wellness Index score (0–100%). WEIGHTED SCORING MODEL Parameter Weight CO₂ 25% PM2.5 25% TVOC 20% Formaldehyde 10% Temperature 10% Relative Humidity 10% Formula: Wellness Index = Σ (Parameter Score × Weight) Each parameter score reflects how closely measurements meet benchmark thresholds.
